Issues

The Broadway Journal was published from January 4, 1845 - January 3, 1846. A 16-page issue was published every Saturday. Charles Frederick Briggs (founder and editor) and John Bisco (publisher) started the journal. Poe contributed from the start, began editing the journal in February 1845, became sole editor in July, and sole owner just a few months later in October.


February 01, 1845
Issue Preview
This issue contains numerous reviews of contemporary literary pieces that were recently published for the American public. The editor's pay particular attention to "A Course of English Reading" by the Rev. James Pycroft, as well as an article titled: "American Prose Writers No. 3" focusing on R.H. Dana. The editors consider Pycroft's work as a valuable educational tool, noting that the work provides the reader with a broad overview of English pedagogy. However, at times Pycroft's assertions do not apply to America, as far as the structure of government is concerned: government's do not for the people, rather the people form the government. (68) The review of the author R.H. Dana, is an editorial focusing on the stylistic and literary talents of the author. The editor's note that Dana, "is a true philosophical observer of life and character," recognized as someone, "beyond his name." (70) The review urges Americans if they are unfamiliar with Dana, to embrace his literary talents. The rest of the issue is spent reviewing the state of American arts, in particular the demise of the American Drama. - Caleb Doan
