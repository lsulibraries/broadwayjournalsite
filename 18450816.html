Issues

The Broadway Journal was published from January 4, 1845 - January 3, 1846. A 16-page issue was published every Saturday. Charles Frederick Briggs (founder and editor) and John Bisco (publisher) started the journal. Poe contributed from the start, began editing the journal in February 1845, became sole editor in July, and sole owner just a few months later in October.


August 16, 1845
Issue Preview

This issue includes several works by Poe: "Lenore," first published in 1843 in Pioneer; the original publications of the poems "The Dream" and "Catholic Hymn"; and, the satirical tale "Never Bet the Devil Your Head: A Tale with a Moral" (85). The latter story was first published in Graham's Magazine in 1841, and begins with Poe facetiously arguing that critics who condemn his stories for their lack of morals simply haven't decided what his morals are yet. He then goes on to rectify this complaint by writing a tale with a clear (and ridiculous) moral: Toby Dammit is a wicked man who frequently "bets the Devil his head," and in the end literally loses his head to the Devil. The piece's sharp note of criticism is carried out in other articles. F. W. Thomas provides an anecdotal biography of John Randolph, characterizing the congressman as arrogant, sharp-tongued, and erratic. He concludes that Randolph's influence despite his inconsistency proves "the power of eloquence" (84). A favorable review of William Hazlitt's commentary on Shakespeare discusses the common critical error of discussing Shakespeare's characters as if they are real people instead of poetic creations. "Fine Arts" condemns cliques within art circles for inhibiting the progress of Art. - Leah Powell
