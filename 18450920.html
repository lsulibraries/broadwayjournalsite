Issues

The Broadway Journal was published from January 4, 1845 - January 3, 1846. A 16-page issue was published every Saturday. Charles Frederick Briggs (founder and editor) and John Bisco (publisher) started the journal. Poe contributed from the start, began editing the journal in February 1845, became sole editor in July, and sole owner just a few months later in October.


September 20, 1845
Issue Preview

Poe's voice is particularly at the forefront of this issue. It contains Poe's "The Landscape Garden" (originally published in the October 1842 Ladies Companion, later retitled "The Domain of Arnheim"), a meta story of a man who-having inherited millions of dollars-decides to become a "Landscape-Gardener." Re-prints of Poe's early unfunny comic story "A Tale of Jerusalem" (first published by Saturday Courier in June 1832) and his poems "To ---" and "Song" (both originally collected in Al Aaraff, Talerlane and Minor Poems in 1829) also appear. In the usual round-up of magazines, Poe compliments the latest issue of The Democratic Review, particularly signaling out Frances Osgood's "Labor" as "one of the finest poems we have yet seen from the most graceful of American poetesses" (168). His sole contempt is reserved for William Jones' "American Humor." Poe corrects Jones' negative assessment of Southern writers Simms and Longstreet. In the "Editorial Miscellany," Poe attends to some extreme cases of plagiarism. Elsewhere, we find the Third Act of Laughton Osborn's "The Magnetizer; or, Ready for Any Body," a play about magnetism and deceptive marriage plots. Mr. Murdock's analysis of British actor Macready, who played Hamlet in London, is quoted at length. - Leah Powell
